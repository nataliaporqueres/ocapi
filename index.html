Project

OCAPi is an ERC-funded project that develops new methods to optimally extract cosmological information from next-generation weak lensing surveys, aiming to investigate the nature of dark energy and the formation of cosmic structures. The project will develop an advanced statistical framework to analyse weak lensing maps at the pixel level, capturing the full information content of the data to provide highly precise constraints on cosmological parameters and to map the matter distribution in the Universe. To achieve this, OCAPi incorporates gravity simulations into a statistical framework that compares simulated and observed lensing maps pixe -by-pixel. This pixel-level approach allows direct comparisons between theory and observations and maximises the scientific return of weak lensing data. OCAPi will apply these methods to data from the Euclid mission, providing a new way to maximise the scientific return of one of the most powerful cosmological datasets.
